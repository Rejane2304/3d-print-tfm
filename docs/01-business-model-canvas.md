## 1. VALUE PROPOSITION

### What problem do we solve?
- Limited access to quality 3D printed products
- Lack of customization in existing products
- Complexity for non-technical users
- High prices in traditional markets

### Unique Value Proposition
> **"Personalized, high-quality, and affordable 3D printed products delivered directly to your door"**

### Differentiating Features
- ✅ Design customization
- ✅ Eco-friendly materials (biodegradable PLA)
- ✅ Competitive prices
- ✅ Diverse catalog (decoration, technical, jewelry)
- ✅ Simplified shopping experience

## 2. CUSTOMER SEGMENTS

3. **Corporate Gifts**

## 9. COST STRUCTURE

### Fixed Costs (Monthly)

| Concept | Estimated Cost |
|---------|----------------|
| Cloud infrastructure | 50-100€ |
| Software licenses | 30-50€ |
| Space rent | 200-500€ |
| Marketing | 100-200€ |
| **Total Fixed** | **380-850€** |

### Variable Costs (Per product)

| Concept | Cost |
|---------|-------|
| Filament | 2-5€ |
| Electricity | 0.5-2€ |
| Packaging | 1-3€ |
| Shipping | 5-10€ |
| Stripe fees | 2-3% |
| **Total Variable** | **15-35% of price** |

### Break-even Analysis
- **Average product price**: 30€
- **Gross margin**: 60%
- **Required monthly sales**: ~30 products

## 📊 Model Summary

### Strengths
- ✅ Low initial cost
- ✅ Gradually scalable
- ✅ Proven technology
- ✅ Growing niche
- ✅ Differentiation through customization

### Weaknesses
- ⚠️ Printer dependency
- ⚠️ Production time
- ⚠️ Technical learning curve
- ⚠️ Limited initial capital

### Opportunities
- 🚀 3D market growth
- 🚀 Sustainability (biodegradable PLA)
- 🚀 On-demand printing
- 🚀 B2B expansion

### Threats
- ⚡ Competition from large platforms
- ⚡ Material cost volatility
- ⚡ Courier dependency
- ⚡ Technical printing failures
